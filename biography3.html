The Biography

Back to Naples and death

Gian Battista Basile

Back in Naples by 1613, because of the prestige he collected in Venice and Mantua, Basile started to be constantly nominated as governor of various neapolitan feuds, most importantly Montemarano (1615) and Avellino (1619-1620), feuds in Basilicata (like Lagopesole and Acerenza, where he took inspiration for many of the fables in Lo Cunto de Li Cunti), and then he was given his most important governorship, the one in Aversa (1629). In 1617 he curated the the first edition for the "Rime" by Galeazzo Tarsia; in 1618 he publishes Aretusa, an idyll dedicated to his protector Marino II Caracciolo, prince of Avellino. The following year he publishes his last theatre work, Il Guerriero Amante.

Basile's death comes at a relatively young age (49 years old) on 22nd February 1632, while he had just been appointed a couple of days prior as governor of the city of Giugliano, because of a violent epidemic of diphteria that swept the neapolitan countryside in this period. He was buried in the Chiesa di Santa Sofia, where his tomb can be still found. His most important works, Le Muse Napolitane and Lo Cunto de Li Cunti (both in the neapolitan language) would be published posthumously between 1634 and 1636, with the pseudonym Gian Alesio Abbatutis.
