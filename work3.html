Pentamerone

The Tales

Following the structure of Boccacio's Decameron, the Pentamerone follows a similar structure. The work is divided in five days, where (taking turns) 10 different women tell each a different story. Additionally there are two more sections, a tale in the tale, at the beginning and the end of the work, acting as a framing device for the Cornice. Moreover, some of the stories, like the last one, also have a reference to the plot of the Cornice itself, acting almost as a meta-theatre device to contextualize the situation the main character, Zoza, has to face. Lastly, in the middle of the book, an Intermezzo can be found, linking the Introduction and the Conclusion of the book. The tales are divided as such:
