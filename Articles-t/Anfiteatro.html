Amphiteatre of Capua

Fifth Day, The Five Sons

The Amphitheatre of Capua was a Roman amphitheatre in the city of Capua (modern Santa Maria Capua Vetere), second only to the Colosseum in size and probably the model for it.[1] It may have been the first amphitheatre to be built by the Romans.[2] and was the location of the first and most famous gladiator school. Today, its remains are found in the comune of Santa Maria Capua Vetere, on Piazza I Ottobre. Much of the stone from the amphitheatre was reused by the Capuans in the Norman period to build the Castello delle Pietre.
